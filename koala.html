KOALA

Amazing Koala Facts:

Koalas are often called “koala bears,” but they're not bears — they're marsupials! That means they carry their babies in a pouch, just like kangaroos.
They sleep up to 18-22 hours a day! Why? Because their food (eucalyptus leaves) is low in energy and takes a long time to digest.You'll only find wild koalas in Australia, mainly in eucalyptus forests along the east coast.

Koalas have strong arms, curved claws, and rough pads on their hands to help them climb and grip trees. They spend almost all their time in trees - even sleeping there!
Koalas have fingerprints that look just like humans' - even under a microscope!In fact, their prints can confuse crime scene investigations.
A baby koala, called a joey, is born the size of a jellybean - about 2 cm long!
Male koalas make deep, bellowing calls to attract mates - surprisingly loud for such a small animal!
