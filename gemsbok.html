GEMSBOK

Gemsbok have long horns that can grow up to 1.2 meters longand they use their horns for defense and to dig for water underground. They are well adapted to life in the dessert, with specialized kidneys that allow them to conserve water.These fascinating animals are well adapted to life in the harsh desert environments of southern Africa.Gemsboks have a special way to keep cool - they can raise their body temperature to avoid sweating and conserve water.Their light-colored coat reflects sunlight, and the dark patches on their face and legs help reduce heat. They are found in the Souther Africa, including South Africa, Namibia and Botswana.

Gemsbok have a large home range, which they mark with scent and dunk to keep other gemsbok away. Their faces have striking black-and-white patterns that help them recognize each other and may even confuse predators. They often live in small herds of 10 to 40 animals, usually led by a dominant female.
Gemsbok are fast and agile runners, able to reach speeds of up to 60km/h.
