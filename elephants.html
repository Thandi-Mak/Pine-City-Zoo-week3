ELEPHANTS

Elephants are highly intelligent and self-aware with a brain similar in structure and function to the human brain. They live in a matriarchal herds, led by the oldest female, and they have strong family bonds.

Elephants are the largest land animals weighing up to 6000kg, and their babies weigh 90,7kg at birth. Their trunks have over 40 000 muscles and are highly versitile, used for breathing, eating, drinking, grasping, and social interactions like hugs.They have an exceptional memory and can remember social relationships, habitats, and migration routes. They can display empathy and cooperation, often helping each other in times of need. Elephants communicate through a range of vocalizations, such as rumbles, roars, and trumpets.

Despite their size, elephants love the water and are exceptional swimmers. They use their trunks as a snorkel when crossing the deep rivers.

African elephants have huge ears shaped like African continent. They flap them like natural fans to cool their bodies.
They can eat up to 136 kilograms of food a day.
An elephant's foot has toe bones hidden inside a big pad.They can walk quietly - like giant ninjas!
