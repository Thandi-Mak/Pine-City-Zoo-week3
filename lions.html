LIONS

The Kings of the Savanna


Lions are symbols of strength, courage, and royalty in many cultures around the world. They're known as the "King of Beasts" for good reason!

Unlike other big cats, lions live in groups called prides. A pride can have up to 30 lions, with moms, cubs, and a few males working together like a team. Female lions (lionesses) do most of the hunting for the pride - they're faster and more agile. Lions use teamwork to hunt large prey like zebras, wildebeest, and even buffalo. They surround and ambush their targets. A true group strategy! The males often protect the pride and join in at mealtime. Lions rest or sleep up to 20 hours a day! They're most active at dawn and dusk, that's when they hunt and patrol.
Male lions grow thick manes around their necks to impress females and intimidate rivals. A darker, fuller mane usually means a stronger and healthier lion. They have the loudest Roar in the Animal Kingdom. A lion's roar can be heard from 8 kilometers away! Roaring helps them communicate, warn rivals, and mark territory.
Lion cubs are born with faint spots that help camouflage them in the grass. The spots usually fade as they grow older.
